# San Diego County Coastal Short-Term Rental Regulations

**Prepared:** 2026-05-20
**Scope:** Every incorporated coastal city in San Diego County, plus the coastal communities inside the City of San Diego.
**Purpose:** Investor-grade reference for Adam Boehrer / Pacific Sotheby's clients evaluating non-owner-occupied STR potential. Every figure should be re-verified by phone or email before a client writes an offer that relies on STR income.

# 1. OCEANSIDE

**Headline:** Non-hosted STRs allowed only west of Coast Highway (Coastal Zone), capped at 480. ~25 slots remaining as of the April 24, 2026 council action. Banned in R-1 zones outside the Coastal Zone since Dec 2023. Permits are non-transferable on sale.

### Allowed?
Hosted (owner on-site as principal residence) allowed citywide in all zoning districts. Non-hosted allowed only in the Coastal Zone, west of Coast Highway. Banned in R-1 outside the Coastal Zone since the Dec 2023 council vote (ordinance effective Feb 10, 2024).

### Geographic Boundaries
- California Coastal Zone (CCC-defined), generally west of Coast Highway.
- R-1 east of Coast Highway: prohibited for non-hosted.
- Existing non-hosted permits in R-1 outside the Coastal Zone are grandfathered but do not renew after a sale, effectively sunsetting.

### Cap, Counts, Waitlist
- 480 non-hosted permit cap (April 24, 2026 council action).
- ~455 permits issued at time of vote, ~25 slots remaining.
- The 480 cap is awaiting California Coastal Commission LCPA certification. The City may not begin enforcing the cap until certification lands.
- Verify slot availability directly with Business License at (760) 435-3878 before any client offers on a non-hosted target.

### Permit Requirements and Operational Rules
- Annual permit fee: $250
- Inspection fee: $207 (initial, then every 3 years)
- TOT: 10% + Oceanside Tourism Marketing District 1.5%–2.5%
- Occupancy: 2 per bedroom + 2 per unit overnight; up to 10 additional daytime guests 7am–10pm; max 5 bedrooms used for STR
- Minimum stay: 2 nights
- Quiet hours: 10pm–10am
- Local contact must respond within 60 minutes to City complaint; 24/7 availability required
- Permit number on all listing platforms; exterior signage with permit #, bedroom count, occupant limit
- Parking: on-site in garage, driveway, or designated spaces

### Renewal and Revocation
Annual renewal; complete renewal due no later than 30 days after expiration. Re-inspection every 3 years. Three verified complaints can trigger modification, suspension, or revocation.

### Transferability on Sale
Permits are non-transferable. New ownership requires a new account and a new permit application. For non-hosted in R-1 outside Coastal Zone, that path is closed entirely (cannot be re-issued).

### Fines and Penalties
- $1,500 first violation
- $2,500 each subsequent
- Two-plus violations trigger an 18-month permit ineligibility

### Recent and Pending Amendments
- Dec 2023: non-hosted STRs banned in R-1 outside Coastal Zone (effective Feb 10, 2024).
- April 24, 2026: council adopted 480 cap west of Coast Highway. Pending Coastal Commission LCPA certification.
- Council is reportedly considering whether hosted units in the Coastal Zone will also need permits under final LCPA.

### City Contacts
- Business License and TOT (primary STR contact): (760) 435-3878, BLinquiry@oceansideca.org
- STR Complaint Hotline (24/7): (760) 435-5460
- Code Enforcement: (760) 435-3944
- Planning and Zoning: (760) 435-3520

# 2. CARLSBAD

**Headline:** Non-owner-occupied STRs allowed only in the Coastal Zone and at the La Costa Resort & Spa Master Plan area (Balboa and Cortez condo buildings at 2003 & 2005 Costa Del Mar Rd). No citywide permit cap. Permits non-transferable on sale.

### Allowed?
Whole-property (non-owner-occupied) STRs permitted only in two areas. Owner-occupancy is not required. Most permissive of the SD coastal markets when the property qualifies.

### Geographic Boundaries
- California Coastal Zone within Carlsbad (generally the western strip).
- La Costa Resort & Spa Master Plan area, specifically the Balboa and Cortez buildings at 2003 and 2005 Costa Del Mar Road (added by a 3-2 council vote in 2016).
- Prohibited everywhere else (most of La Costa, Bressi Ranch, non-coastal Aviara, La Costa Valley, etc.).

### Cap and Waitlist
No cap, no waitlist. City publishes an interactive map of all permitted STVRs (address, emergency contact, issue/expiration dates).

### Permit Requirements and Operational Rules
- STVR Permit Fee: $381 plus $100 first-time registration
- Annual renewal fee: $225 (for permits applied for after Sept 1, 2024)
- Business License: ~$118–$265
- TOT: 10% + Carlsbad Tourism Business Improvement District: $1/room/night (some sources cite 2%)
- Application package (CMC 5.60): Form P-98 (STVR application) + Form P-98A Impact Response Plan
- Occupancy: 2 per bedroom (or per studio) + 1 per unit
- Local contact: 24/7 availability, 45-minute response time to complaints
- Permit number on all rental listings
- Neighbor notification required (part of Impact Response Plan)
- No minimum-night requirement specified in 5.60 (one of the few NSD cities without one)

### Renewal and Revocation
Annual renewal. Three administrative citations or verified code violations within 24 months trigger permit revocation for at least 36 months. Administrative citation structure: $100 / $200 / $500 escalating.

### Transferability on Sale
Permits do not transfer to new owners. New owner must apply, and the property must still be in the eligible zone (generally yes by location, but the buyer goes through fresh application).

### Fines and Penalties
- Administrative citations $100 / $200 / $500 escalating
- 3 strikes in 24 months: 36-month revocation
- Operating without a permit subject to separate enforcement

### Recent and Pending Amendments
2024 fee restructure ($225 annual renewal effective Sept 1, 2024) is the most recent material change. No public council action on a cap or geographic expansion in 2025-2026.

### City Contacts
- Community Development, STVR Program: STVR@CarlsbadCA.gov, (442) 339-5978
- Code Enforcement also accepts complaints

# 3. ENCINITAS (incl. Leucadia, Cardiff-by-the-Sea, Olivenhain, Old Encinitas, New Encinitas)

**Headline:** Non-hosted STRs allowed citywide subject to caps. New ordinance approved by California Coastal Commission Feb 5, 2026 with modifications. Citywide cap ~654 non-hosted (2.5%), Coastal Zone cap ~376 (4% west of I-5). 2-night minimum, 200-ft spacing between non-hosted STRs.

### Allowed?
Yes, citywide. Both hosted and non-hosted permitted. Hosted is exempt from caps and minimum-night rule.

### Geographic Boundaries
- All five communities (Leucadia, Cardiff-by-the-Sea, Old Encinitas, New Encinitas, Olivenhain) covered by the same ordinance.
- Citywide cap: 2.5% of residential units, ~654 non-hosted permits.
- Coastal Zone (west of I-5) sub-cap: 4%, ~376 non-hosted permits.
- Seabluffe (private community, NW Encinitas): exempt from the cap. 57 of 255 units historically operated as STRs and are counted separately.

### Cap, Counts, Waitlist
New caps approved by Coastal Commission Feb 5, 2026. Exact "currently issued" count at the cap effective date not yet posted publicly. Confirm with Development Services before advising. Application opens online through the city's Customer Self-Service (CSS) portal. ~2-week concurrent review by Planning, Code Enforcement, Finance.

### Permit Requirements and Operational Rules
- Permit term: 2 years (longer than most peer cities)
- First permit fee: $815
- Renewal fee: $598
- Minimum stay (non-hosted): 2 nights (Coastal Commission reduced the city's proposed 3-night minimum to 2)
- 200-foot rule: non-hosted STRs must be ≥200 ft from another non-hosted STR
- Hosted STRs: no minimum stay, no cap
- TOT required. Local contact, signage, occupancy, parking standards apply per ordinance (Ord. 2022-15 / 2022-16 are the controlling instruments).

### Renewal and Revocation
2-year permit cycle. Subject to citation and revocation per code. Specific trigger counts not in public summaries, confirm with Development Services.

### Transferability on Sale
Standard CA municipal pattern is non-transferable (each owner re-applies). Confirm directly with Development Services for any specific deal.

### Fines and Penalties
Citation structure per municipal code; specific dollar amounts not publicly summarized.

### Recent and Pending Amendments
Feb 5, 2026: California Coastal Commission approved Ord. No. 2022-15 and 2022-16 with two modifications (the major one being 3-night to 2-night minimum). Effective date follows city's acceptance of modifications and re-adoption.

### City Contacts
- Development Services, STR Program: permits@encinitasca.gov, (760) 633-2708
- Apply via Customer Self Service (CSS) portal at encinitasca.gov

# 4. SOLANA BEACH

**Headline:** STRs allowed in all residential zoning districts but minimum stay is 7 consecutive days. Effectively eliminates the typical Airbnb weekend-rental investor model. No citywide cap, no owner-occupancy requirement. City Council added STR review to its work plan May 21, 2025; changes may be coming.

### Allowed?
Yes, in all residential zoning districts (single-family, duplex, condo, twinplex, townhome, multi-family). ADUs excluded.

### Geographic Boundaries
Permitted in all residential zones citywide. No coastal-zone vs. non-coastal split.

### Cap and Waitlist
None. No waitlist.

### Permit Requirements and Operational Rules (SBMC Chapter 4.47)
- Minimum stay: 7 consecutive days. Rentals under 7 days prohibited in all residential zones. Only one booking per 7-day period.
- Permit fee: identical to business certificate fee (current amount on the STVR application form, verify with Finance). Annual permit.
- TOT rate: 13% (notably higher than Oceanside/Carlsbad's 10%)
- Owner-occupancy not required

### Renewal and Revocation
Annual renewal. Revocation triggers per SBMC 4.47, specific trigger counts not exposed on the public summary.

### Transferability on Sale
Not publicly summarized; expect non-transferable per CA municipal norm. Confirm with city.

### Fines and Penalties
Per SBMC 4.47 citation structure, confirm dollar amounts via Code Compliance.

### Recent and Pending Amendments
City Council May 21, 2025 meeting (item C.2) added review of STVR regulations to its work plan. The city has acknowledged complaints (noise, parking, unruly guests) and "is evaluating existing STVR Permit regulations for modifications." Watch this; stricter rules likely incoming over 2026–2027.

### City Contacts
- Code Compliance / Code Enforcement (primary STR contact): (858) 720-2413, CodeCompliance@cosb.org
- Finance (TOT/billing): (858) 720-2460, Finance@cosb.org

# 5. DEL MAR

**Headline:** Non-owner-occupied STRs prohibited. STRs allowed only in primary residences where the owner lives 6+ months/year. Citywide cap of 129 permits with neighborhood sub-caps (77 North Beach / 32 South Beach / 19 Hills). 150 existing STRs grandfathered. No new permits available; waitlist via Microsoft Forms. Effective March 2026; existing-STR application window closed May 1, 2026.

### Allowed?
Effectively no for typical investor (non-owner-occupied) STRs. Ordinance restricts STRs to primary residences. Hosted STRs (owner home, renting a room) exempt from cap and minimum-night rule.

### Geographic Boundaries
Citywide, subdivided into:
- North Beach area: max 77 permits
- South Beach area: max 32 permits
- Hills area: max 19 permits

Apartment STRs allowed only in commercial zones. Condominiums STRs capped at 10% of total units across residential and commercial zones.

### Cap, Counts, Waitlist
- Citywide cap: 129 permits (5% of housing stock)
- Existing STRs grandfathered: 162 applied by Dec 31, 2024 deadline, 150 confirmed as Existing STRs. These exceed the cap.
- No new permits available until count drops below 129.
- Waitlist via Microsoft Forms on the city's STR portal.
- Existing STR permit application window: March 2 to May 1, 2026 (now closed).

### Permit Requirements and Operational Rules
- Primary residence required (owner lives there 6+ months/year)
- Permit term: 2 years
- First permit fee: $815
- Renewal fee: $598
- Minimum stay: 3 nights
- Hosted rentals: no caps or minimum
- TOT required per Del Mar Municipal Code Ch. 3.12, remitted through Rentalscape
- 24/7 STR Public Facing Portal for complaints
- Sheriff non-emergency: (858) 868-3200

### Renewal and Revocation
2-year cycle. Grandfathered existing STRs continue until permit expires, is not renewed, or property is sold.

### Transferability on Sale
Permits do not transfer on sale. Sale of a grandfathered STR property means the new owner loses grandfathered status and must qualify as a primary residence with cap availability. This is the most hostile market in San Diego County for non-owner-occupied investing.

### Fines and Penalties
Per Code Enforcement Division. Specific dollar amounts not publicly summarized.

### Recent and Pending Amendments
- Sept 2024: Del Mar City Council passed the ordinance (5-0 vote, 129 cap)
- Feb 2026: California Coastal Commission approved with modifications
- March 2 to May 1, 2026: Existing STR permitting window

### City Contacts
- Planning and Community Development: planning@delmar.ca.us
- San Diego County Sheriff (non-emergency): (858) 868-3200

# 6. CITY OF SAN DIEGO (STRO Program)

**Headline:** City of San Diego operates a 4-tier STRO licensing system. Whole-home non-owner-occupied rentals legal only under Tier 3 (citywide, ~5,400 cap, ~880 remaining) or Tier 4 (Mission Beach, 1,097 cap, 0 available, waitlist closed). Licenses are non-transferable on sale; this is the single most important fact for investor buyers.

### Governing Authority
SDMC Chapter 5, Article 10, Division 1, §§510.0101–510.0114. Operation without license unlawful as of May 1, 2023. Coastal Commission certified the ordinance March 9, 2022.

### Tier Structure
- Tier 1: Whole home OR home share, up to 20 rental nights per calendar year, no cap, $193 license fee. Useful only for occasional rentals.
- Tier 2: Home sharing, host resides on-site as primary residence (max 90 days/year absent), no cap, $284 license fee. Owner-occupied only.
- Tier 3: Whole-home, host absent, outside Mission Beach. Capped at ~1% of total citywide housing units per SDMC §510.0107. Most coastal neighborhoods (La Jolla, Pacific Beach, Ocean Beach, Point Loma, Sunset Cliffs, La Playa, Bay Park, Crown Point) are Tier 3.
- Tier 4: Whole-home, host absent, Mission Beach Community Planning Area only. Capped at 30% of Mission Beach housing units (~1,097 issued, cap reached).

### Caps, Counts, Lottery, Waitlist

| Tier | Cap | Issued (May 2026) | Available |
|------|-----|-------------------|-----------|
| 1 | Unlimited | 141 | Unlimited |
| 2 | Unlimited | 2,363 | Unlimited |
| 3 | ~1% citywide (~5,400-5,600) | 4,750 | ~856 |
| 4 | 30% Mission Beach (~1,097) | 1,097 | 0 |

Tier 4 lottery: most recent application window July 1 to August 15, 2025; results published by August 22, 2025. New waitlist published on STRO site. No new lottery scheduled. Waitlist positions reviewed quarterly; licenses release only on revocation or forfeiture.

Tier 3: application window has remained open with rolling issuance up to the cap. When cap hits, applicants enter a waitlist reviewed quarterly. Still accepting applications as of May 2026 but the window is closing fast (~95% issued).

"Good Actor" prioritization (applies in lottery scenarios): active TOT registration and remittance, 90+ nights booked per calendar year, no verifiable code violations in prior 2 calendar years, bonus weight for hosts who rented 2+ years and 90+ nights before October 2021.

### Permit Requirements

All tiers require: TOT Certificate (free, via Treasurer), active Rental Unit Business Tax (RUBT) account, and if host is not owner, Business Tax Certificate plus right-to-occupy document. Application via Accela Citizen Portal.

Fees effective March 1, 2025:

| Tier | App Fee | License Fee | Total Initial | Renewal Total | Term |
|------|---------|-------------|---------------|---------------|------|
| 1 | $33 | $193 | $226 | $226 | 2 yrs |
| 2 | $33 | $284 | $317 | $317 | 2 yrs |
| 3 | $41 | $1,129 | $1,170 | $1,170 | 2 yrs |
| 4 | $41 | $1,129 | $1,170 | $1,170 | 2 yrs |

TOT: 10.5% of gross rental revenue including cleaning fees. Airbnb/VRBO collect and remit on behalf of most hosts; direct bookings require self-remittance.

### Operating Requirements (all tiers)
- Minimum stay: 2 nights (Tiers 1, 3, 4). Tier 2 also 2 nights when host-absent in home-share scenario.
- Tier 3 and 4 utilization: minimum 90 days actually rented per year, quarterly reporting required, or face revocation.
- Occupancy limit: 2 persons per bedroom + 2 additional (must be posted).
- Good Neighbor Policy provided to all guests.
- Local contact must respond in person or by phone within 1 hour of complaint. Cannot delegate to out-of-area answering service.
- Posting: 8.5"×11" exterior signage visible from sidewalk, 20-pt black bold all-caps, listing STRO license #, TOT certificate #, host/local contact info, code enforcement contact.
- Parking: no oversized vehicles (>27 ft length OR >7 ft height) on-site.
- Trash off-street except collection days.
- Noise 24-hour enforcement; dual citations to host and guest possible.
- Human trafficking training documentation required, kept on file.
- One license per person citywide under any ownership structure.
- ADUs built after October 15, 2017 are prohibited from STR use (kills the "add an ADU and rent it on Airbnb" play).

### Renewal and Enforcement
License term 2 years; renewal same fee schedule. Three-strike rule: 3 substantiated complaints in any 12-month period triggers license suspension. Revocation triggers include failure to maintain 90-day annual utilization (Tier 3/4), fraudulent application (permanent disqualification), repeat violations. Hosting platforms legally required to verify valid STRO license before activating any San Diego listing.

### Transferability on Sale
CRITICAL: Licenses are NOT transferable. License is tied to the host (individual), not the property. When a property sells, the existing license is extinguished or stays with prior host. New owner must apply independently. If buyer wants Tier 3 in a capped scenario, they go to the back of the waitlist. If buyer wants Tier 4, they cannot apply; waitlist closed.

### Fines and Penalties
Up to $1,000 per violation administrative citation. Dual citations possible (effective $2,000 per noise event). Police response cost recovery. Escalating penalties for repeat offenders. Administrative action up to and including license revocation and permanent disqualification.

### Coastal Commission Status
STRO ordinance certified March 9, 2022. No new STRO-specific Coastal Commission amendment pending in the 2026 docket per the latest San Diego Coast District Deputy Director's Report (Feb 2026). Active LCP amendments in San Diego coastal zone in 2025-2026 focus on ADU regulations, not STRO.

### Coastal Neighborhood Detail (City of San Diego)

- **La Jolla** — Tier 3, citywide pool. No additional overlay. High-end nightly rates make Tier 3 economically attractive while available.
- **Pacific Beach** — Tier 3, citywide pool. Historically highest complaint volume per capita; expect heightened code enforcement scrutiny. PB Town Council has lobbied for tighter restrictions, no neighborhood carve-out yet.
- **Mission Beach** — Tier 4 exclusive. Cap reached, waitlist closed since Aug 2025. Existing STRO license does not transfer with property sale; buyer cannot get a new Tier 4 license. Deal-killer for any investor buyer pricing in STR income at acquisition.
- **Ocean Beach** — Tier 3. Heavily residential, active OB Town Council, complaint-sensitive.
- **Point Loma / Sunset Cliffs** — Tier 3. Sunset Cliffs is within Coastal Overlay Zone; building or remodel projects face CCC Coastal Development Permit review, but STRO licensing itself is City-administered.
- **La Playa** — Tier 3. Within Point Loma, Coastal Overlay Zone applies for development.
- **Crown Point** — Tier 3, important distinction. Crown Point sits on Mission Bay but is part of the Pacific Beach Community Planning Area, NOT the Mission Beach Community Planning Area. Tier 4 does NOT apply. Easy to misread because of proximity.
- **Bay Park** — Tier 3. Inland of I-5, fully citywide pool, no coastal overlay implications.

# 7. CORONADO

**Headline:** Coronado effectively bans STRs citywide. Minimum rental period 26 consecutive days in all residential zones. Penalties up to $2,500 per violation. No tier system, no permit path. This market is closed to STR investors.

### Governing Authority
CMC Chapter 86.78 (Transient Occupancy). In effect for decades; ordinance survived a 2019 lawsuit, settled in city's favor.

### Allowed?
No. CMC §86.78 prohibits use of any dwelling or habitable unit in residential zones as a "transient rental." A transient rental is defined as occupancy of 25 consecutive days or less; 26 nights is the absolute minimum legal rental period.

### Caps / Lottery
None. Coronado does not run a permit cap, lottery, or waitlist. There is no legal STR program to opt into.

### Permit Requirements
N/A. Owners of rentals 26+ days fall under standard Coronado Transient Occupancy Tax framework via CMC Title 86 and CMC Ch. 86.34 (updated by Ordinance No. 2025-03), but these are mid-term and long-term categories.

### Enforcement
City actively monitors Airbnb/VRBO/Booking.com listings. Owners receive notice of violation; non-compliance escalates to civil suit. The 2019 Coronado litigation set the precedent the City still cites.

### Fines and Penalties
Up to $2,500 per violation per CMC Ch. 1.10 (Code Enforcement Administrative Fines). Civil penalties, court costs, attorney fees. Successive daily violations possible.

### Coronado Cays Note
Title 90 of CMC covers the Coronado Cays Specific Plan. Research did not find a confirmed STR carve-out for the Cays. Treat the 26-night minimum as applicable throughout Coronado until verified directly with city Planning.

### City Contacts
- Coronado City Hall: 619-522-7300, 1825 Strand Way, Coronado, CA 92118
- Code Enforcement / Planning: via main line; staff directory at coronado.ca.us/Directory.aspx

# 8. IMPERIAL BEACH

**Headline:** STRs legal only in commercial/mixed-use zones, never in residential zones. Effectively confines STR investment to a narrow corridor of Palm Avenue, Seacoast Drive, and Imperial Beach Boulevard. No cap, no lottery, but no residential STR is possible.

### Governing Authority
IBMC Title 18/19 (Zoning) for zone restrictions; IBMC Chapter 3.24 for TOT.

### Allowed Zones
- C/MU-1 (General commercial mixed-use)
- C/MU-2 (Seacoast commercial mixed-use)
- C/MU-3 (Neighborhood commercial mixed-use)
- C/R-ET (Commercial/recreational ecotourism)

Geographic footprint: Palm Avenue, Imperial Beach Blvd corridor, Seacoast Drive, 13th Street / Imperial Boulevard area.

Residential zones (R-1-3800, R-1-6000, R-3000, R-3000-D, R-2000, R-1500): STR prohibited. Parcel zoning determines everything.

### Cap, Lottery, Waitlist
No cap, no lottery, no waitlist. Permit available to any property within allowed zones.

### Permit Requirements
- Business license required, category "Property Rental – Short Term"
- Application 100% online via Imperial Beach Citizen Self-Service (CSS) Portal
- TOT: 14% of gross rental revenue (increased from 10% Jan 1, 2023). Significantly higher than San Diego's 10.5%.
- "Transient" = anyone occupying ≤30 consecutive days (IBMC §3.24)
- TOT filing due last day of month following collection
- Application fee not published; confirm with Finance
- Coastal Permit required for any project not exempt per IBMC §19.87.040 (applies if within Coastal Zone)

### Renewal and Enforcement
Business license renewed annually. Late TOT: 10% penalty, +10% if >30 days delinquent, +0.5% monthly interest.

### Transferability
Not explicitly documented in IB code. Business licenses generally do not transfer in California cities. Confirm with IB Finance/Community Development before structuring any acquisition.

### Fines and Penalties
$1,000 for operating without business license. TOT late penalties as above. Civil enforcement available.

### City Contacts
- Community Development (zoning, STR eligibility): 619-628-1358
- Finance (TOT, business license): 619-628-1365, adminservices@imperialbeachca.gov
- Hours: Mon-Thu 7:30 AM to 5:30 PM
- Address: 825 Imperial Beach Boulevard, Imperial Beach, CA 91932

# Investor Takeaways

1. **Carlsbad's Coastal Zone is the most permissive investor market** of the SD coastal cities. No cap, no minimum nights, clear rules. Constraint is finding a property in the Coastal Zone or the La Costa Resort exception (Balboa/Cortez condos at 2003/2005 Costa Del Mar Rd).

2. **Oceanside has ~25 non-hosted permits remaining** in the Coastal Zone before the cap closes (pending CCC certification). Move-fast buyers should be coached that permit availability is the gate, not the property. R-1 properties outside Coastal Zone are dead for non-hosted regardless of past use.

3. **City of San Diego Tier 3 window is narrow** (~880 licenses left citywide as of April-May 2026). If a client wants any San Diego coastal STR (La Jolla, PB, OB, Point Loma, Crown Point, Bay Park), they should apply before close, not after.

4. **Mission Beach Tier 4 is closed and licenses don't transfer.** Any Mission Beach listing claiming "STRO included" is selling a license the buyer cannot inherit. Pricing in STR income at acquisition without an independent path to a license is a material risk.

5. **Del Mar is closed to absentee investors.** Primary-residence requirement plus 129-cap plus waitlist. Stop pitching Del Mar as an STR investment market.

6. **Solana Beach's 7-day minimum kills the typical Airbnb model** but works for monthly snowbird rentals. Council actively reviewing; expect tightening.

7. **Encinitas is in a regulatory transition** (new ordinance took effect Q1 2026). The 200-ft spacing rule means dense pockets (Leucadia 101 corridor, Cardiff coastal blocks) will hit the spacing cap quickly. Confirm available "slots" at the specific address with city staff before any client offer.

8. **Coronado is closed.** 26-night minimum, full stop. Anyone advertising sub-26-night rentals is operating illegally and faces $2,500/violation.

9. **Imperial Beach STR is a zoning play.** The only way in is buying in C/MU-1/2/3 or C/R-ET. Most of IB's residential housing stock is ineligible.

10. **Permits are non-transferable in every jurisdiction surveyed.** Never tell a buyer "the property comes with the STR permit." The buyer must qualify, the property must be eligible, and in capped cities a slot must be open.

11. **TOT rates differ meaningfully** — IB 14%, Solana Beach 13%, San Diego 10.5%, Oceanside 10% + 1.5-2.5%, Carlsbad 10% + $1/night. Material impact on net yield modeling.

12. **All info above should be verified by phone before a buyer writes an offer that relies on STR income.** Public ordinance pages lag council actions by weeks to months.
