## How To Read This Report

Every nightly rate, occupancy figure, and revenue projection in this document is sourced from a named platform with a clickable link. Where two or more platforms disagree, both numbers are shown so you can see the spread yourself. The model layered on top of the data uses three operator scenarios (conservative, realistic, strong) so you can pick the assumption set that matches how aggressively you'd run the property.

Two structural facts that matter before you read the numbers:

1. **No major STR data provider publishes Terramar as its own neighborhood.** AirDNA, AirROI, Rabbu, and Key Data report Carlsbad at the city level. Terramar performance is inferred from city benchmarks plus actual nightly rates of comparable Terramar listings.
2. **Terramar HOA caveat:** the private beach landing is restricted such that sub-30-day rental guests cannot independently use it. This is documented in the Terramar Association buyer brief on file. It does not stop short-term rental, but it does reduce the marketable amenity set vs an unrestricted oceanfront listing.

## Regulatory Layer — Carlsbad STR Operating Costs

All sourced directly from the City of Carlsbad and confirmed via [Allura Homes Carlsbad STR guide](https://www.allurahomes.com/shortterm-rental-regulations/comprehensive-guide-to-operating-an-airbnb-or-short-term-rental-in-carlsbad-california):

| Cost | Amount | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Carlsbad business license | $118–$265 | Annual |
| Short-Term Vacation Rental permit | $381 + $100 first-time fee | Annual |
| Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT) | 10% | Per booking, paid by guest |
| Carlsbad Tourism Business Improvement District (CTBID) | 2% | Per booking, paid by guest |

### Operating requirements (Carlsbad Municipal Code Ch. 5.60)
- Must be in Coastal Zone or La Costa Resort area (5303 Los Robles is in the Coastal Zone — STR is permitted)
- Maximum occupancy: **2 per bedroom + 1** (so 3BR = 7 max, 2BR ADU = 5 max)
- Local contact must respond within **45 minutes**
- Impact Response Plan required at permitting
- Annual permit renewal required
- Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit TOT and CTBID directly to the city (since Feb 2020 for Vrbo)

### Terramar HOA caveat
Per the Terramar Association buyer brief on file:
- **Sub-30-day rental guests cannot independently use the private beach landing.**
- This means the private landing access cannot be marketed as a guest amenity for typical short-term stays.
- Workarounds: host escorts guests, or list as 30+ day mid-term rental (which Carlsbad data suggests can match net STR revenue in 92011 after accounting for lower cleaning frequency and reduced regulatory overhead).
