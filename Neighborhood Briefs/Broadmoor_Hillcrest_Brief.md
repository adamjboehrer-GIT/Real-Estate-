# Broadmoor & Hillcrest — Central San Clemente Field Brief

*Built for Adam Boehrer, Sotheby's International Realty, San Clemente. Verify any active comps in CRMLS before quoting in a listing presentation.*

---

## BROADMOOR

**Tract identity and era.** One of the four classic "Central San Clemente Hill" tracts, alongside Pacifica, Rancho Margarita, and Hillcrest. Most of the housing stock dates to the **mid-1970s**, situated on the rise just up the hill from San Clemente High School off Avenida Presidio. Unlike the master-planned communities to the east, Broadmoor was built out as a conventional single-family tract, non-gated, no HOA. The "older San Clemente" character that buyers now actively seek as a counterweight to Talega.

**Street network.** The bones run on Avenida Salvador as the spine, with Aragon, Madrid, and Alcazar threading west toward the ridge. Shorter Calles (Lazo, Empalme, Tobarra) are the cul-de-sac and single-loaded streets that give Broadmoor its quiet, residential feel. Note: per marketing-voice rules, never name Adam's specific street in any public-facing copy. "Broadmoor / central San Clemente" is the right level of specificity.

**Lots, footprints, plans.** Predominantly **single-level ranch homes**, 3 to 4 bedrooms, 2 baths, 2-car attached garage, with floor plans typically **1,900 to 2,330 sq ft**. Lots tend to be **oversized** for Orange County standards, especially compared to anything built post-1990 in Talega or Forster Ranch. Yards are generous front and back. Some two-story plans exist but the single-story dominance is what differentiates Broadmoor from Hillcrest's more mixed inventory.

**Topography and views.** Broadmoor sits on the ridge that drops west toward El Camino Real and the coast. Western edges of Salvador and the upper portions of Aragon and Alcazar carry **whitewater and Catalina ocean views**; interior streets get hill and city-light views. Elevation also delivers a real microclimate edge.

**Pico Promenade connectivity.** Avenida Pico is the artery the neighborhood runs to for daily life. Within a 5-minute drive: **Plaza Pacifica** (Walmart, Pavilions, Lowe's, Office Depot, Michaels), **Pico Plaza** (99 Store, Fitness 19, Stuft Pizza, Del Taco), and the surrounding restaurant cluster. Note: there is no shopping center named "Pico Promenade" in the city. The corridor is colloquially referred to that way but the actual centers are Plaza Pacifica and Pico Plaza. Worth getting that right in any flyer copy.

**Ridgeline access.** Broadmoor's signature amenity. The **Avenida Salvador trailhead** at 721 Avenida Salvador (the water-tower entry) is the southern access point of the Rancho San Clemente Ridgeline Trail. Walking-distance trail access is the single most underweighted lifestyle feature in any Broadmoor listing. Most Talega and Forster Ranch buyers have to drive to a trailhead.

**Schools.** Capistrano Unified. The bulk of Broadmoor feeds **Concordia Elementary** (3120 Avenida del Presidente, the closer campus on the southwest side) for K to 5, then **Shorecliffs Middle** (240 Via Socorro), then San Clemente High. The Vista Del Mar K-8 boundary is east of Avenida Pico in Talega/Forster Ranch territory. Confirm any specific address on capousd.org's boundary lookup before writing it into a listing. Boundaries shift and Capo realigns periodically.

**Comp range.** As of early 2026, the **citywide median is roughly $1.6M** with $/sqft around **$775 to $777**. Broadmoor itself, given the mid-70s vintage and large lots, generally trades **at or modestly below the citywide median per square foot** on unrenovated stock and **above it for view-corridor or remodeled** product. Pull comps within 0.25 mi of subject and screen for view orientation before quoting a number.

**Owner profile.** Mix of **long-tenure originals** (40+ years, paid-off, often the ARM-balloon-aside seller pool already targeted via PropStream filters) and **second-wave move-ups** who upgraded into Broadmoor in the 2003 to 2007 and 2019 to 2021 windows. The long-tenure cohort is the highest-leverage outreach group: high equity, no loan friction, often considering Idaho/Arizona/Tennessee relocation.

## HILLCREST

**Tract identity and era.** Older than Broadmoor. Built by **Pacesetter Homes in the mid-1960s**, making Hillcrest the senior of the four central tracts. Pacesetter was the same builder responsible for Shorecliffs, so the construction lineage and quality benchmark is shared. Floor plans run roughly **1,510 to 2,042 sq ft**, 3 to 4 bedrooms, 2 baths, 2-car garage, mix of one- and two-story.

**Street network.** Calle Hidalgo, Calle Borrego, Calle Vivienda are the workhorse residential streets. The Avenida Vista Hermosa / Avenida Vista Montana corridor sits on the northern edge, transitioning toward Forster Ranch. Important distinction: locals sometimes blur "Hillcrest" with parts of Forster Ranch and Forster Highlands, but the original Pacesetter tract is the older, smaller-footprint inventory. Forster Highlands and the Reserve are 1980s to 2000s, larger, often with no Mello-Roos.

**"Hillcrest Estates" question.** No formal subdivision called "Hillcrest Estates" surfaces in CTC's San Clemente Subdivision Map or in the major neighborhood guides. The "Estates" label gets used loosely by listing agents for the larger view lots on Avenida Vista Hermosa and Vista Montana. Treat it as marketing language, not a recorded tract name. The meaningful distinction is **Hillcrest proper (Pacesetter, 1960s)** vs. **Forster Highlands / The Reserve (1980s to 2000s, no Mello-Roos)** vs. **Forster Ranch (1980s to 2009 broader area)**.

**Topography and views.** Ridge-adjacent, similar story to Broadmoor: north-side and west-side lots can carry ocean and Catalina views; interior streets get hills, sunsets, and city lights. The elevation again delivers the same microclimate advantage.

**Trails and parks.** Walking distance to the Rancho San Clemente Ridgeline Trail northern reaches and to the Forster Ranch trail network. Closest large parks: **Forster Ranch Community Park** at 3207 Camino Vera Cruz (locally "Pirate Park," multipurpose fields, tennis, jungle gym, picnic), **Vista Hermosa Sports Park** at 987 Avenida Vista Hermosa (AYSO and youth soccer hub), and **Richard T. Steed Memorial Park** at 247 Avenida La Pata (the regional anchor: pool, skate park, tournament fields).

**Schools.** **Truman Benedict Elementary** at 1251 Sarmentoso (K to 5, Capo USD), then **Bernice Ayer Middle** at 1271 Sarmentoso (the two share a campus block), then San Clemente High. This Truman Benedict / Bernice Ayer pairing is one of the strongest school stories in San Clemente. Both are top-decile within Capo USD and the K-8 walkability is a distinct selling point Hillcrest has over Broadmoor (which feeds Concordia / Shorecliffs further south).

**Comp range.** Hillcrest's older inventory historically traded at lower absolute prices than Broadmoor. Older guides peg the band in the $600K to $1M range, but those guides are stale. **Real-world early-2026 Hillcrest sales are running at or above the $1.0M to $1.4M range for unrenovated 1,500 to 2,000 sq ft homes** and breaking $1.5M+ for view product or full renovations. Pull live comps before quoting.

**Owner profile.** Heavier original-owner concentration than Broadmoor given the mid-60s vintage. Estate-sale and probate inventory has historically been a meaningful slice of Hillcrest turnover. Pair this with the seller-signal logic in CLAUDE.md targeting framework. Long tenure + high equity + 60s tract = strong list.

## COMPARATIVE THEMES

**The Ridgeline Trail.** The Rancho San Clemente Ridgeline Trail runs roughly **3.5 miles one-way (about 5.7 miles out-and-back)** with around 780 ft elevation gain. Six entry points, three with parking. The official trailhead is at Avenida San Pablo near Calle Ameno, but for Broadmoor and Hillcrest residents the practical entries are the **Avenida Salvador water-tower trailhead** (Broadmoor) and the northern access points off Avenida Vista Hermosa / Avenida Pico (Hillcrest side). Views span Dana Point Headlands south to north San Diego County. Used hard by runners, mountain bikers, equestrians, and dog walkers. It functions as the social spine of these two neighborhoods. Best lifestyle differentiator versus Talega's interior trails.

**San Clemente High School draw.** Tritons. **Around 2,765 students 9 to 12**, ranked top 20% of California public high schools, top 254 statewide on Public School Review's 2026 list, top 1,386 of 9,523 California schools. 91.9 percentile statewide. Strong sports culture. Football program and surf team are the two banner identities, and the school's surf legacy is a real recruiting story for relocating coastal-CA families. Both Broadmoor and Hillcrest sit walking-or-biking distance to campus, which is rare for San Clemente generally.

**Coastal-side SC vs. these inland tracts.** Sea Pointe Estates, Cyprus Shore, Cotton Point, Riviera District. Coastal Southwest SC trades on **direct ocean access, surf culture (Trestles, T-Street), and gated luxury** at $3M to $10M+ price points. Broadmoor and Hillcrest sell on **value-per-square-foot, lot size, school walkability, and trail access** at roughly half to one-third the coastal price. They are not competing for the same buyer. They convert different buyer profiles entirely.

**Talega comparison.** The clean test case. Master-planned, HOA-governed, **active Mello-Roos CFD assessments running $4,000 to $10,000+/year** depending on parcel and CFD vintage. Newer construction, smaller lots, golf course community, manicured. Broadmoor and Hillcrest are the **anti-Talega**: no HOA, no Mello-Roos on the original tracts, larger lots, older homes with character, and walkable to high school and trails rather than requiring a car for everything. The buyer who picks Broadmoor or Hillcrest over Talega is usually someone who has lived in Talega or seen it and rejected the carrying-cost math, OR who specifically wants the older-SC neighborhood feel.

**Forster Ranch comparison.** Closer cousin to Hillcrest than Talega is. Forster Ranch homes were built **early 1980s through 2009**, larger plans than Hillcrest, the newer pockets (Forster Highlands, The Reserve) are **no-Mello-Roos**. Hillcrest has the **schools edge** (Truman Benedict + Bernice Ayer share the same Sarmentoso campus block); Forster Ranch has the **floor-plan-size edge** for buyers wanting 2,500+ sq ft.

**Drive times (verify in Waze before quoting).** From Broadmoor / Hillcrest center: around 5 min to I-5 at the Avenida Pico interchange, 7 to 10 min to the San Clemente Pier, 10 to 12 min to the Outlets at San Clemente, around 15 min to Dana Point Harbor.

**Where residents actually go.** Daily errands: Plaza Pacifica (Pavilions, Walmart). Coffee: Bear Coast on Pico, Stride on Del Mar, Ellie's Table downtown. Restaurants: South of Nick's, Nick's San Clemente, OC Brewing Co., Sundried Tomato, Pizza Port. The Talega / Vista Hermosa restaurant cluster (Avila's, Sushi 369) pulls some traffic too.

## INSIDER NOTES — "WHAT LOCALS KNOW"

**Microclimate.** The marine layer settles hard on the coastal bluffs (Riviera, Pier Bowl, Capo Beach) on May/June mornings. Broadmoor and Hillcrest sit on the inland ridge, often **above the fog line** by mid-morning, and run a few degrees warmer in winter and noticeably sunnier in late spring. A real lifestyle delta worth naming on flyers. Same reason buyers choose North Carlsbad over coastal Carlsbad applies here. Best framed as "many residents notice..." rather than as a hard-data claim.

**Best view streets.** In Broadmoor: the western reaches of Avenida Salvador, the upper end of Avenida Aragon, and the south-facing lots on Calle Tobarra. In Hillcrest: the northern stretches of Avenida Vista Montana and Avenida Vista Hermosa, and the higher elevations of Calle Hidalgo and Calle Vivienda. Always confirm view from the actual lot. View is preserved or destroyed by neighboring trees and any single new build can alter the calculus.

**School drop-off chokepoints.** SCHS opens onto Avenida Pico. Pico backs up 7:45 to 8:15 AM and 2:45 to 3:15 PM. Truman Benedict / Bernice Ayer drop-off on Sarmentoso bottlenecks on Camino Vera Cruz. Local move: cut down Camino Mira Costa or Calle Frontera to bypass.

**Coastal rail / bluff erosion story — the competitive advantage.** The LOSSAN rail corridor through the San Clemente bluffs has been closed five times in the past three years. The April 2026 closure suspended Metrolink and Amtrak service for around 6 weeks for emergency stabilization, with a 1,400-foot catchment wall under construction at Mariposa Point through Summer 2026. Long-term solutions including inland tunneling are under study at $4 to $6B estimates. **None of this affects Broadmoor or Hillcrest.** These neighborhoods sit on the inland ridge, far from the coastal bluff failure zone, and the only real exposure is the loss of train service into LA (which is already a workaround everyone has adjusted to). For an out-of-state buyer or an investor evaluating San Clemente, this is a clean, factual "inland ridge isn't the bluff" story that protects long-term value.

**HOA / Mello-Roos.** Both tracts are **non-HOA, no Mello-Roos** in their original Pacesetter (Hillcrest) and 1970s (Broadmoor) cores. Confirm parcel-by-parcel on the title sheet before writing it into a marketing claim. Newer infill or refis can attach Community Facilities District obligations.

**Cross-promotion angle for the planned neighborhood guide.** Both neighborhoods sell on the same five things, in roughly this order:
1. **Walkability to SCHS and the Ridgeline Trail**
2. **No HOA / no Mello-Roos / large lots**
3. **Inland-ridge climate above the marine layer**
4. **Older San Clemente character vs. master-planned alternatives**
5. **Value per square foot** vs. coastal SC

Lead with those five. Don't lead with views. Views are the differentiator between specific lots, not between Broadmoor/Hillcrest as a category.
