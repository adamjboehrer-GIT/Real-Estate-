## 2. Sender, cadence, and infrastructure recommendations

- **Sender identity in copy:** "I'm Adam Boehrer, working with Sam Schmidt's team on press for his new memoir, *No Finish Line*…" (signals rep status without claiming to be Sam).
- **Reply-to:** Adam's chosen domain (see #4 above).
- **Volume cap:** No more than 30 sends/day from a single mailbox in the first two weeks to protect deliverability. ~350 contacts → roughly 12 days of staged sends at that cap.
- **Sequencing strategy** (see §6 for detailed week-1 plan):
  - **Tier A first**, vertical by vertical, personalized — not blasted.
  - **No follow-up automation until week 2.** Hand-watch reply rates.
  - **Follow-up cadence:** +3 business days for no-reply on Tier A, +5 on Tier B, single follow-up only.
- **Tracking:** Log every send + outcome in a simple sheet (date, outlet, contact, vertical, status, notes). I can stand this up as a Google Sheet once #4 is decided.
- **Killswitch:** If any single contact replies negatively about being pitched, pause sends across that outlet for 30 days. Reputation > volume.

## 5. Disability framing — the two-voice split (do not cross the wires)

The disability-justice audience actively rejects "supercrip" / "overcoming" framing. Pitches to Wong, Shapiro, Luterman, Morris, Ruder, Pushing Limits, Down to the Struts → **Template C, hard-news voice**, leading with Sam's blunt quotes about access inequality and $40M of research funding. **Never** open with the wedding-dance or the exoskeleton imagery to this segment — it lands as inspiration porn and we lose them.

The faith and human-interest TV audience (Joni, Hartman, Goalcast, Upworthy, NBC Inspiring America, daytime TV) → **Template D**, leading with the wedding dance and the son narrating the audiobook. Both are true; the choice is which beat the recipient's audience expects.

In the CSV the agent tagged every disability contact with their supercrip-tolerance level. Trust that flag when filling templates.
