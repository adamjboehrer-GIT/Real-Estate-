# Market Moves — May 2026

**Source:** California Regional MLS via InfoSparks (ShowingTime Plus). Residential, Single Family.
**Reporting month:** May 2026 (latest closed data; compared to April 2026 and to May 2025).
**Pulled:** June 8, 2026.

---

## The headline

Inventory is **up month over month across all four markets**, the normal spring build as more sellers come on. But it is still **well below where it was a year ago** in every one of them. So when a client asks "is inventory up or down," the honest answer is: up from last month, still tight versus last year. The supply squeeze that has defined this market has not gone away, it is just easing seasonally.

Orange County overall sits at 2.9 months of supply, firmly a seller's market. The three coastal cities split: San Clemente and Dana Point are tight, Laguna Beach is the outlier and is genuinely a buyer's market right now.

A note on the flow numbers below: May pending and closed sales appear to still be settling in the MLS (pendings dropped double digits in every market at once, which usually means late status updates rather than a real one-month collapse). Lean on active listings and months of supply as the solid read this month, and treat May pendings as soft.

---

## Orange County (countywide benchmark)

- **Inventory:** 3,126 active listings, up 5.3% from April (2,969), down 19.0% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 2.9, up from 2.7 last month, down from 3.6 a year ago. Seller's market.
- **Pace:** median 12 days on market, essentially flat (12 a year ago).
- **Price:** median $1,467,500, up 1.2% month over month, up 5.6% year over year. Steady.
- **Pricing power:** homes selling at 100% of last list price.
- **New supply:** 1,435 new listings, down 14.3% from April.

**The story:** A healthy, balanced-to-tight county. Supply is rebuilding off very low levels but demand is still absorbing it fast and prices are grinding up at a sustainable mid-single-digit pace.

## San Clemente

- **Inventory:** 98 active listings, up 22.5% from April (80), down 36.8% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 2.8, up from 2.2 last month, down from 3.5 a year ago. Seller's market.
- **Pace:** median 8 days on market, the fastest of the four. Down from 11 last month and 14 a year ago.
- **Price:** median $2,237,000, up 9.1% month over month, up 10.5% year over year.
- **Pricing power:** 100% of last list price.
- **New supply:** 59 new listings, up 13.5% from April.

**The story you can tell a client:** San Clemente is the tightest, fastest market of the group. Inventory jumped this month but is still down more than a third from last year, well-priced homes are gone in about a week, and sellers are getting full asking. This is a seller's market where pricing right means multiple looks fast.

## Dana Point

- **Inventory:** 72 active listings, up 4.3% from April (69), down 28.7% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 3.6, up from 3.3 last month, down from 4.4 a year ago. Balanced, leaning seller.
- **Pace:** median 10 days on market, fast. Down from 12 last month.
- **Price:** median $3,450,000, up 15.2% month over month, up 40.8% year over year. **Flag this one:** Dana Point's median is volatile because the monthly transaction count is small, so a few high-end closings swing it hard. The +40.8% is a mix-shift spike, not a real 40% appreciation. Frame it as "the high end is active," not as a price jump.
- **Pricing power:** 99.4% of last list price.

**The story you can tell a client:** Dana Point is balanced and tilting toward sellers. Homes are still moving in about 10 days. The headline median is being pulled up by luxury closings, so use the days-on-market and supply numbers, not the price, to describe momentum.

## Laguna Beach

- **Inventory:** 153 active listings, up 2.0% from April (150), down 32.0% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 7.4, up slightly from last month, down from 12.3 a year ago. **Buyer's market.**
- **Pace:** median 35 days on market, by far the slowest of the four.
- **Price:** median $3,380,000, up 2.7% month over month, up 6.5% year over year.
- **Pricing power:** 97.2% of last list price, the softest of the group, so there is real negotiating room.

**The story you can tell a client:** Laguna Beach is the one buyer's market in the set. At 7.4 months of supply, with homes taking over a month to sell and trading about 3% under list, buyers have leverage here that they do not have in San Clemente or Dana Point. For a seller in Laguna, pricing discipline matters more than anywhere else on this list. Worth noting supply is still down a third from last year, so it is a buyer's market by local standards, not a soft one by absolute standards.

---

## What changed vs last month (the 3 that matter)

1. **Inventory ticked up everywhere** (San Clemente most, +22.5%), the seasonal spring build. Still down 19% to 37% year over year in every market.
2. **San Clemente got faster and pricier**, 8 days on market and a 9% month-over-month median bump, the standout tight market.
3. **The coastal split is clear:** San Clemente and Dana Point are seller's markets moving fast, Laguna Beach is a buyer's market at 7.4 months of supply. Same coastline, very different leverage.
