# Market Moves — May 2026

**Source:** California Regional MLS via InfoSparks (ShowingTime Plus). Residential, Single Family.
**Reporting month:** May 2026 (latest closed data; compared to April 2026 and to May 2025).
**Pulled:** June 16, 2026 (refresh of the June 8 pull; May data has settled further, so these numbers supersede the earlier ones).

---

## The headline

Inventory is **up month over month in three of the four markets**, the normal spring build as more sellers come on. But it is still **well below where it was a year ago** in every one of them, down 20% to 37%. So when a client asks "is inventory up or down," the honest answer is: up from last month, still tight versus last year. The supply squeeze that has defined this market has not gone away, it is just easing seasonally.

Orange County overall sits at 2.8 months of supply, firmly a seller's market. The three coastal cities split: San Clemente and Dana Point are tight, Laguna Beach is the outlier and is genuinely a buyer's market right now.

A note on the flow numbers below: May pending and closed sales appear to still be settling in the MLS (pendings dropped double digits in every market at once, which usually means late status updates rather than a real one-month collapse). Lean on active listings and months of supply as the solid read this month, and treat May pendings as soft.

---

## Orange County (countywide benchmark)

- **Inventory:** 3,058 active listings, up 2.8% from April (2,974), down 20.7% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 2.8, up from 2.7 last month, down from 3.6 a year ago. Seller's market.
- **Pace:** median 12 days on market, essentially flat (11 last month, 12 a year ago).
- **Price:** median $1,467,500, up 1.2% month over month, up 5.6% year over year. Steady.
- **Pricing power:** homes selling at 100% of last list price.
- **New supply:** 1,483 new listings, down 12.2% from April.

**The story:** A healthy, balanced-to-tight county. Supply is rebuilding off very low levels but demand is still absorbing it fast and prices are grinding up at a sustainable mid-single-digit pace.

## San Clemente

- **Inventory:** 97 active listings, up 22.8% from April (79), down 37.4% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 2.7, up from 2.1 last month, down from 3.5 a year ago. Seller's market.
- **Pace:** median 8 days on market, the fastest of the four. Down from 11 last month and 14 a year ago.
- **Price:** median $2,237,000, up 9.1% month over month, up 10.5% year over year. The single-month jump runs hot, so lean on the roughly 10% year-over-year figure as the truer read.
- **Pricing power:** 100% of last list price.
- **New supply:** 64 new listings, up 23.1% from April.

**The story you can tell a client:** San Clemente is the tightest, fastest market of the group. Inventory jumped this month but is still down more than a third from last year, well-priced homes are gone in about a week, and sellers are getting full asking. This is a seller's market where pricing right means multiple looks fast.

## Dana Point

- **Inventory:** 74 active listings, up 5.7% from April (70), down 26.7% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 3.6, up from 3.3 last month, down from 4.4 a year ago. Balanced, leaning seller.
- **Pace:** median 9 days on market, fast. Down from 12 last month.
- **Price:** median $3,450,000, up 15.2% month over month, up 40.8% year over year. **Flag this one:** Dana Point's median is volatile because the monthly transaction count is small (only 23 closings), so a few high-end sales swing it hard. The +40.8% is a mix-shift spike, not a real 40% appreciation. Frame it as "the high end is active," not as a price jump, and do not quote the 40% figure.
- **Pricing power:** 99.4% of last list price.

**The story you can tell a client:** Dana Point is balanced and tilting toward sellers. Homes are still moving in about nine days. The headline median is being pulled up by luxury closings, so use the days-on-market and supply numbers, not the price, to describe momentum.

## Laguna Beach

- **Inventory:** 148 active listings, down 2.6% from April (152), down 34.2% from a year ago.
- **Months of supply:** 6.9, flat for the month, down from 12.3 a year ago. **Buyer's market.**
- **Pace:** median 35 days on market, by far the slowest of the four. Up from 25 last month.
- **Price:** median $3,380,000, up 2.7% month over month, up 6.5% year over year.
- **Pricing power:** 97.2% of last list price, the softest of the group, so there is real negotiating room.

**The story you can tell a client:** Laguna Beach is the one buyer's market in the set. At 6.9 months of supply, with homes taking over a month to sell and trading about 3% under list, buyers have leverage here that they do not have in San Clemente or Dana Point. For a seller in Laguna, pricing discipline matters more than anywhere else on this list. Worth noting supply is still down a third from last year, so it is a buyer's market by local standards, not a soft one by absolute standards.

## What changed vs last month (the 3 that matter)

1. **Inventory ticked up in three of four markets** (San Clemente most, +22.8%), the seasonal spring build. Laguna edged down. Still down 21% to 37% year over year everywhere.
2. **San Clemente got faster and pricier**, 8 days on market and a 9% month-over-month median bump, the standout tight market.
3. **The coastal split is clear:** San Clemente and Dana Point are seller's markets moving fast, Laguna Beach is a buyer's market at 6.9 months of supply. Same coastline, very different leverage.
