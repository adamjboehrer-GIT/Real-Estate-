# Short-Term Rental Regulations — Coastal SoCal

**Scope:** Every coastal city from San Clemente north to Redondo Beach.
**Lens:** Non-owner-occupied, full-time vacation rentals (the pure investor thesis — buyer never lives in the home).
**Date:** May 20, 2026.

Every section ends with an investor verdict and the realistic-target / no-go geography. Permit caps, waitlists, transferability, and Coastal Commission posture are called out explicitly because those four variables decide whether the investment thesis works.

---

## The Two Variables That Decide Everything

1. **Is there a legal path for a non-owner-occupied STR at all?** Many cities have effectively closed this — host must be primary resident, or STR is banned outright, or only commercial zones qualify.
2. **Does the permit transfer with the property on sale?** This is the single biggest underwriting variable. A non-transferable permit is not an asset — it's a personal license that dies at conveyance. Newport Beach is the lone "permit transfers freely" city in this corridor; that's the entire reason permitted Newport Beach STRs trade at a premium.

A third variable is rising fast in 2025–2026: **the Coastal Commission overhang**. Under *Keen v. Manhattan Beach* (2022 appellate) and the *Hermosa Beach* trial-court ruling (2024, not appealed by Hermosa in 2026), city STR bans inside the Coastal Zone that haven't been certified by the Coastal Commission are likely unenforceable. This creates speculative legal openings in RPV and Redondo Beach — but those are litigation plays, not passive rentals.

## City-by-City (South to North)

### 1. San Clemente

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** Yes, but only inside four designated visitor zones (North Beach, Downtown, Pier Bowl, South El Camino corridor). R-1 single-family outside those overlays is off-limits.
- **Cap:** 20% density cap per eligible zone. No fixed citywide number — once a zone hits 20% saturation, no new STLU permits issue there.
- **Waitlist:** No formal waitlist; gating is cap-based. If your zone is full, you wait for attrition.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** Treat as **non-transferable** for underwriting. STAR permits explicitly die at sale; STLU permits require a recorded change-of-ownership covenant, strongly implying the city will re-evaluate. Verify with Planning before close.
- **TOT:** 10%.
- **Minimum nights:** Unsettled (3 vs 5 night debate hasn't finalized).
- **Investor verdict:** **Hard, address-specific.** Only condos and small SFRs inside the four overlay zones, only if that sub-area is under the 20% cap. Pier Bowl is typically at or near cap. Talega, Forster Ranch, Marblehead, Rancho San Clemente, Hillcrest, Broadmoor — **no.**

### 2. Dana Point

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** Yes — explicitly defined "Non-Primary STR" tier exists.
- **Cap:** 60 non-primary citywide. Layered caps: 115 Coastal Zone total, 115 Non-Coastal Zone total. Coastal Zone is at/near full; Non-Coastal Zone has headroom (was at 59 of 115 in July 2024; 15 more approved May 2025).
- **Waitlist:** Yes — annual reapplication required to hold your spot. Coastal Zone is the bottleneck; Non-Coastal Zone now auto-clears under staff-level approval.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **Non-transferable.** Ordinance is explicit. New owner must reapply.
- **TOT:** 10%.
- **Minimum nights:** 2 consecutive.
- **Investor verdict:** **The most plausible target city in OC for a non-owner-occupied STR.** But the non-transferability is a real underwriting hit — assume a 60–90 day post-close income gap and a non-zero chance the buyer never gets re-permitted at all. Best targets are Non-Coastal Zone inland Dana Point parcels (Del Obispo, inland Lantern Village, inland Capistrano Beach). Coastal Zone deals (Monarch Beach, oceanside Capo Beach, Headlands) face full caps and waitlists.

### 3. Laguna Beach

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No** in residential zones (R-1, R-2, R-3). Pre-October-2020 permits are grandfathered but **die at sale**. Only commercial and mixed-use zones (LB/P, C-N, C-1, CH-M, CBD-1, CBD-2) accept new STR permits.
- **Cap:** 300 STLU citywide + ~165 separate home-share (owner-occupied) slots.
- **Waitlist:** Not the operative gating mechanism — residential is a flat zoning prohibition, commercial is capped.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **Non-transferable.** Permits tied to owner. Residential grandfathered units extinguish at sale.
- **TOT:** 12% (verify).
- **Investor verdict:** **Effectively impossible for a residential investor play.** Any Laguna SFR or condo in R-1/R-2/R-3 — Top of the World, Bluebird Canyon, Three Arch Bay, Emerald Bay, Mystic Hills, Arch Beach Heights, the Village — cannot be permitted for STR by a new owner. **Watch out for listings marketed as "STR permitted"** in residential zones — the buyer cannot inherit it. The only viable path is commercial/mixed-use along the highways or in the downtown specific plan, which is a different real-estate product entirely.

### 4. Newport Beach (including Balboa Peninsula, Balboa Island, Corona del Mar, Newport Coast)

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **Yes** — and Newport Beach is structurally the most STR-friendly city in this corridor for investors.
- **Zoning:** Permitted in R-1.5, R-2, RM, plus newer MU-W2 and MU-CV/15th Street mixed-use zones. **R-1 single-family is excluded** for new permits (pre-June-2004 permits in R-1 are grandfathered and still transferable). Newport Coast is technically eligible by zone in some places but HOAs near-universally prohibit stays under 30 days. Lido Isle: same HOA story. Newport Island has a separate 20-permit owner-on-site sub-cap.
- **Cap:** **1,550 citywide active permits.** Effectively full. New permits only open via attrition (no TOT filed for 2 consecutive years = abandoned).
- **Waitlist:** Yes, first-come/first-served. Adding your address does not guarantee a permit. Plan on years, not months. New 2026 rule limits each permit to one reinstatement (vs. unlimited before) — should slowly accelerate attrition.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **YES — the permit transfers with the property** if the buyer files within 60 days of close, and that transfer is **not counted against the cap.** This is the entire investor thesis: you buy the permit with the property.
- **TOT:** 10%.
- **Minimum nights:** 2 consecutive. Guests must be 25+.
- **Annual renewal:** $336 (2026, up from $214) + business license (~$260).
- **Investor verdict:** **Plausible — uniquely so in this corridor — but only via acquisition of a permitted property.** Realistic targets: Balboa Peninsula, Balboa Island, eligible-zone CdM parcels, all **with active transferable permits**. Expect a permit premium baked into the price. Verify the permit is active on the city dashboard, TOT is current, and structure escrow so transfer paperwork goes in immediately at close. Newport Coast and Lido Isle: skip — HOAs block STR even where city zoning allows.

### 5. Huntington Beach

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **Almost universally no.**
  - **Zone 1 (all of HB except Sunset Beach):** Owner-occupied only. No non-owner-occupied permits, no grandfathering, no exceptions.
  - **Zone 2 (Sunset Beach):** Non-owner-occupied permits were allowed but only for owners registered by March 1, 2022. **Closed since.**
- **Cap:** No numeric cap on owner-occupied. De facto closed cap on unhosted in Sunset Beach.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **Non-transferable** — and this is the killer. A grandfathered Sunset Beach unhosted permit **dies at sale**. The buyer cannot inherit it.
- **TOT:** 10% + 6% Tourism BID = ~16% total tax burden on guest stays.
- **Investor verdict:** **Skip Huntington Beach entirely for any non-owner-occupied investor STR thesis.** No viable path anywhere in the city, and Sunset Beach's grandfathered permits don't survive a transaction.

### 6. Seal Beach

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** Yes, but only in the Coastal Zone (Old Town Seal Beach and the small coastal area), and only within a **47-permit citywide hard cap**. Properties outside the Coastal Zone (Leisure World, College Park, The Hill, Bridgeport) are ineligible.
- **Cap:** 47 total. 14 pre-existing CUPs grandfathered and run with the land; the remaining ~33 were initially allocated via lottery on May 10, 2023.
- **Waitlist:** Lottery + waitlist hybrid. Initial allocations are likely fully claimed; turnover rate not published.
- **Permit transfer on sale:**
  - **Grandfathered CUPs (the 14): transfer with the property.** Most valuable STR-eligible parcels in the city.
  - **Lottery permits: ambiguous** — confirm with city.
- **TOT:** 8% or 12% — sources conflict; verify with Finance.
- **Investor verdict:** **Hard, bordering on impossible** — but a narrow real path exists if you can buy one of the 14 grandfathered-CUP Old Town parcels when it lists. Treat as a Newport-style "buy the permit with the property" play with a universe of 14.

### 7. Long Beach

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **Yes — explicitly.** Non-Primary Residence registration tier under LBMC Ch. 5.77 with unlimited rental days.
- **Cap:** 800 non-primary citywide. **At least 350 reserved for the Coastal Zone** (Belmont Shore, Naples, Belmont Heights, Alamitos Beach, Downtown shoreline) — that floor is required by the Coastal Commission and gives investors a structurally protected sub-allocation.
- **Waitlist:** Yes once cap is full. Current count vs. cap is not published — direct call to `ShortTermRentals@longbeach.gov` to confirm before quoting timelines.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **Ambiguous in public sources.** Default assumption for underwriting: it does NOT transfer until confirmed in writing by the LB STR office.
- **TOT:** 13% (verify; some sources say 12%).
- **The investor killer to understand — the petition opt-out:** Any census block can opt itself out of non-hosted STRs if >50% of homes sign a petition. Annual opportunity. Filer pays ~$1,050 mailing fee. A block that's legal today can be illegal in 12 months if neighbors organize. 200+ home blocks have already been targeted.
- **Investor verdict:** **Plausible with caveats.** Coastal Zone (Belmont Shore, Naples, Alamitos Beach) is the strongest target — the 350-unit Coastal Commission floor offers protection no other city in this corridor has. East/North Long Beach blocks carry standing petition-organizing risk that needs to be priced in. Three pre-purchase confirmations are mandatory: (1) waitlist length, (2) does the permit transfer, (3) is the target block facing any petition.

### 8. City of Los Angeles — San Pedro & Wilmington

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No.** LA's Home-Sharing Ordinance (LAMC 12.22 A.32) requires the host to occupy the property as primary residence (183+ days/year). Citywide rule — applies to San Pedro, Wilmington, Venice, Pacific Palisades, everywhere.
- **Cap:** None numeric, but the eligibility gate (primary residence only) self-limits.
- **Permit transfer on sale:** **Cannot transfer** — buyer can't inherit the seller's primary-residence status.
- **TOT:** 14% + ~$3.20/night enforcement fee.
- **Penalties:** $2,000/day or 2x nightly rate, plus up to 3-year ban from future registration.
- **Investor verdict:** **Effectively impossible** for a pure non-owner-occupied STR anywhere in the City of LA, including San Pedro and Wilmington. The only legal paths are: (a) owner actually moves in and runs Extended Home-Sharing, (b) 31+ day mid-term/corporate furnished rental which falls outside the STR ordinance entirely, or (c) niche grandfathered apartment-house plays (Venice Suites-type) — not a buy-side strategy. Tell clients clearly: if their thesis depends on Airbnb income in San Pedro or Wilmington as a non-resident, the thesis is not viable under current law.

### 9. Palos Verdes Estates (PVE)

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No. Outright ban.** Ordinance O19-739 (Oct 22, 2019) prohibits STRs in all residential zones AND prohibits advertising them.
- **Cap:** Zero permits issuable.
- **Coastal posture:** PVE has limited Coastal Zone exposure (most of the city sits above the bluff line), so the *Keen v. Manhattan Beach* legal opening that exists for some neighbors largely doesn't apply.
- **Enforcement:** Active. Resident advocacy group (Keep Quiet PVE) reports violators.
- **Investor verdict:** **Don't pursue.** Any listing marketed in PVE with "Airbnb potential" is operating in violation of city code. Only legal furnished-rental path is 31+ day stays.

### 10. Rancho Palos Verdes (RPV — separate from PVE)

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No** in all residential zones (RS, RM). Outright prohibition since Ord. 592U/593 (2016-2017).
- **Fines:** $2,500 / $5,000 / $7,500 escalating per citation. Advertising independently prohibited.
- **Coastal Zone wrinkle — THE only opening:** RPV's STR ban is NOT in the certified Local Coastal Program. Under *Keen v. Manhattan Beach* (2022 appellate) and the Hermosa Beach trial-court ruling (2024, not appealed in 2026), RPV's ban inside the Coastal Zone (bluff frontage south and west — portions of Portuguese Bend, Abalone Cove, Marymount, Trump National area, Point Vicente) is **likely unenforceable**. No RPV-specific court ruling exists yet.
- **Investor verdict:**
  - **Inland RPV (non-Coastal Zone — most of the city):** No. Ban is valid, enforced, fines stack fast.
  - **RPV Coastal Zone:** **Litigation play, not a passive rental play.** An investor here would be the test case — needs legal counsel willing to challenge enforcement and financial runway to absorb $2,500–$7,500 citations. Most clients should treat RPV as a no-go.

### 11. Torrance

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No in residential zones.** STRs are only legal as a "home share" with the host on-site during each rental. Unhosted STRs are permitted only in commercial zones.
- **Coastal posture:** Torrance's ordinance IS Coastal-Commission-certified (CDP 5-20-0031, 2020), so unlike RPV and Redondo, the Torrance rule has legal force in the Coastal Zone (the small Torrance Beach frontage). No *Keen* opening.
- **Cap / Waitlist / Transferability:** Not published clearly. Likely no numeric cap, likely non-transferable per California norm. Confirm with Torrance Planning (310-618-5990) before underwriting.
- **TOT:** 11%.
- **Investor verdict:** **Skip.** Hollywood Riviera and other desirable Torrance beach-adjacent neighborhoods are residential and host-on-site is mandatory — that eliminates the investor model. Only commercial-zoned parcels along PCH or Hawthorne work, and those aren't the typical product an investor is looking at.

### 12. Redondo Beach

- **Non-owner-occupied STR allowed?** **No.** Redondo bans STRs in all residential zones (R-1, R-1A, R-2, R-3, R-3A, RMD, RH-1, RH-2, RH-3) via a clever drafting move: it defines any rental under 30 days as a "hotel/motel" and then doesn't list hotel/motel as a permitted use in residential zones.
- **Coastal Zone wrinkle — same as RPV:** Redondo's STR prohibition has **NOT** been certified by the Coastal Commission. The city's current Focused General Plan Update (Aug-Sep 2024 comment period) hasn't certified the STR ban specifically. Under *Keen* and Hermosa precedent, the ban is **almost certainly unenforceable in the Coastal Zone** (west of PCH/Catalina — Riviera Village, the Esplanade, the Avenues, South Redondo). No Redondo-specific court ruling yet.
- **Enforcement:** Active — cease-and-desist letters, business license revocation.
- **Investor verdict:**
  - **Inland Redondo (east of PCH):** No.
  - **Coastal Zone Redondo:** Same posture as RPV Coastal Zone — likely unenforceable but no court ruling. Litigation play, not a passive rental.
  - **Commercial / mixed-use parcels along PCH or in the Pier area:** Possible if zoned for hotel/motel use, but not single-family-home investor product.

## The Practical Top of the List

If a client walks in saying "I want to buy a SoCal coastal STR-permitted investment property between San Clemente and Redondo Beach," in priority order:

1. **Newport Beach** — buy a permitted Peninsula, Balboa Island, or eligible CdM property. The permit transfers. This is the only city in the corridor where you can transact your way into an STR business without going on a waitlist.
2. **Long Beach Coastal Zone** — Belmont Shore, Naples, Alamitos Beach. 350-permit Coastal Commission floor is a real moat. Confirm transferability and check for active petition activity in the target block.
3. **Dana Point Non-Coastal Zone** — staff-level approvals post-May-2025 means real headroom. But underwrite a re-permitting gap and tail risk on the buyer's eventual resale.
4. **Seal Beach** — only if a grandfathered-CUP Old Town parcel lists. Universe of 14 total.
5. **San Clemente overlay zones** — if the parcel is inside one of the four eligible areas AND that sub-area is under the 20% cap.

Everything else (Laguna residential, HB, LA, PVE, RPV, Torrance residential, Redondo) is either prohibited, gated by host-on-site, or a litigation play.

## Items That Should Be Confirmed Before Any Specific Offer

These are the calls Adam (or the client's attorney) needs to make on a deal-by-deal basis. Caps move, court rulings drop, ordinances get amended.

- **San Clemente:** Is the target parcel inside an STLU-eligible zone, and is that zone under the 20% cap? Will the STLU permit transfer? — Planning Division (949-361-6197 / planning@san-clemente.org).
- **Dana Point:** Confirm zone (Coastal vs Non-Coastal), confirm current cap headroom, confirm new owner can secure a fresh permit on a reasonable timeline.
- **Laguna Beach:** Confirm parcel zoning (any residential = unworkable). Don't be sold on a "grandfathered" residential permit — it doesn't transfer.
- **Newport Beach:** Verify the permit is active on the city dashboard, TOT is current, transfer paperwork structured to file within 60 days of close. Verify HOA CC&Rs (especially Newport Coast and Lido Isle).
- **Long Beach:** Waitlist length and current count vs 800 cap, permit transferability in writing, target block's petition history.
- **Seal Beach:** Whether the target is one of the 14 grandfathered CUPs or a lottery-allocated permit, and whether the latter transfers.
- **RPV / Redondo Coastal Zone:** APN-level Coastal Zone confirmation, current city enforcement posture, legal counsel willing to litigate.
