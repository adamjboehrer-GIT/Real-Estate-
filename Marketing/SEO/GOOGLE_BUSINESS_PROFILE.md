# Google Business Profile — Setup Walkthrough (Adam Boehrer)

This is the single highest-ROI thing you can do for local search. It controls whether you
show up in the Google map pack and in "real estate agent near me" results around San Clemente.
It is free and it lives outside the website (so I cannot do it for you — it needs your Google
login). Budget 30 minutes once, then 5 minutes a week for reviews.

## Why it matters more than the website itself
For local searches, Google leans on the map pack (the 3 businesses with the map pin) far more
than the blue links below it. A complete, active, well-reviewed profile is what gets you in
that pack. Most solo agents never finish theirs, so this is winnable.

## Step 1 — Claim or create the profile
1. Go to **google.com/business** and sign in with the Google account you want tied to this
   (your adamjboehrer@gmail.com is fine).
2. Search your name + "Pacific Sotheby's San Clemente." If a profile already exists (Sotheby's
   may have created one), click **Claim this business**. If not, **Add your business**.
3. Business name: **Adam Boehrer — Pacific Sotheby's International Realty**.
4. It will mail or call/text a verification code. Enter it to confirm you own the listing.

## Step 2 — Fill it out completely (Google rewards 100% complete)
- **Primary category:** Real Estate Agent. (Add secondary: Real Estate Consultant.)
- **Service area:** Add each city you work — San Clemente, Dana Point, Laguna Beach, Newport
  Beach, Oceanside, Carlsbad, San Diego. (Agents typically use a service-area profile rather
  than a public street address.)
- **Phone:** 949.541.8247
- **Website:** https://adamboehrer.com
- **Appointment link:** https://adamboehrer.com/#home-value  ← points people straight at the
  home value report form, so the profile itself becomes a lead source.
- **Hours:** set realistic hours (even "by appointment").
- **Photos:** add your headshot, a few sharp coastal/property shots (follow the brand photo
  rules — no filters, no B&W, no text overlays), and the Pacific Sotheby's logo. Profiles with
  photos get materially more clicks.
- **Services:** add "Home valuation / CMA," "Buyer representation," "Listing / seller
  representation," "Market analysis."
- **From the business / description:** reuse the site voice. Lead like a neighbor:
  > A friendly neighborhood real estate agent on the South Orange County coast. I help buyers and
  > sellers from San Clemente to San Diego, and I will give you an honest read on what your home
  > is actually worth today. Request a free, personal home value report at adamboehrer.com.

## Step 3 — Reviews (this is the real engine)
Reviews are the biggest ranking factor in the map pack, and they convert. Goal: a steady drip,
not a one-time blast. Text past clients and your sphere individually (personal > mass).

Send them the direct review link (in your profile dashboard under **Ask for reviews** / "Get
more reviews" — it looks like `g.page/r/...`). Suggested text:

> Hi [name], quick favor. I'm building out my Google profile and a short review from you would
> mean a lot, it really helps people find me. Takes about a minute: [your review link]. Thank you!

After a closing, ask while the good feeling is fresh. Reply to every review (even a thank-you on
the 5-stars) — Google likes active profiles.

## Step 4 — Keep it warm
- Post a "Google Post" every couple of weeks (a market stat, a new listing, the home value
  offer). You can reuse Coastal Currents content.
- Whenever a home value report or newsletter issue goes out, that's a natural moment to also
  drop a Google Post.
