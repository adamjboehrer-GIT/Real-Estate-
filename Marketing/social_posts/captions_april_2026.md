## Post 1 — "15 days"

**Image:** `sc_15_days_linkedin.jpg` (1200x1200) and `sc_15_days_instagram.jpg` (1080x1350)

### LinkedIn caption

> 15 days. That's the median time it took to sell a home under $5M in San Clemente in March. Half a month from list to close.
>
> For context: that pace is faster than the spring norm anywhere in the country, and faster than most of South OC right now. Dana Point sat at 74 days, Newport Beach at 149, Laguna Beach at 201.
>
> Two things behind it. Inventory is tight, and the homes that come on are priced where the market actually is. Only 28% of active SC listings have a price reduction, one of the lowest rates in the county.
>
> Buyers should be ready to move quickly and bid cleanly. Owners thinking about listing have rare leverage, but only if the first asking price is right.

## Post 2 — "101.1%"

**Image:** `sc_sale_to_list_linkedin.jpg` (1200x1200) and `sc_sale_to_list_instagram.jpg` (1080x1350)

### LinkedIn caption

> 101.1%. That's the average sale-to-list ratio for San Clemente homes under $5M that closed in March. Buyers are paying at or above asking on the homes that are priced right.
>
> The split matters. Under $5M: 101.1% sale-to-list, 15-day median DOM. $5M and above: 97.2% sale-to-list, 74-day median DOM. The ultra-luxury tier is negotiating harder. Below it, the market is rewarding decisive offers.

> If you're shopping the under-$5M band here, "list price" is the floor on a quality home, not the ceiling. If you're on the $5M+ side, the leverage is back on your side of the table.

## Post 3 — "What I'm Watching" (LinkedIn, text only, no image)

> What I'm watching this cycle: the share of San Clemente listings that have taken a price reduction.
>
> It's at 28%. Among the lowest in Orange County. For comparison, Laguna Niguel is at 37%, Newport Coast 35%, Irvine 34%, Laguna Beach 34%.
>
> The read: SC sellers are pricing right the first time. That isn't true everywhere. In markets where a third or more of listings have already cut, you're watching a delayed correction work its way through the data. In SC right now, the homes that come on are landing close to where the market is willing to pay.
>
> It also means a price reduction in SC stands out more than it would in a peer market. If a home cuts here, that's a signal worth paying attention to.
>
