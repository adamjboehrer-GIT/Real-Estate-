## Week of June 1, 2026 (ISO week 23 · rotation slot 3)

### Post 1 — Tue June 2 · Pillar: Market data · STATUS: DRAFT

> San Clemente is sitting on 2.2 months of housing supply right now. A year ago it was 3.6.
>
> Months of supply is just how long it would take to sell every active listing at the current
> pace of sales. Anything under three months is a seller's market by the textbook, and 2.2 is
> well inside that line. You can feel it in the speed: the median home went from list to sold in
> 12 days last month, down from 16 a year ago, with 44 new homes going under contract in April
> against 73 active listings.
>
> Here is the part that gets missed. 36% of active listings in town are already carrying a price
> cut. Tight inventory does not mean anything sells. It means the homes priced where the market
> actually is move fast, and the ones reaching for a number get left behind to chase it down.
>
> If you are buying here, be ready to move cleanly on the right home. If you are weighing a sale,
> the leverage is real, but only if the launch price is honest.
>
> Full breakdown across Newport, Laguna, Dana Point, San Clemente, Oceanside, and Carlsbad in this
> week's Coastal Currents. Link in profile.

### Post 2 — Thu June 4 · Pillar: Seller education · STATUS: DRAFT

> The most expensive mistake a South OC seller can make has nothing to do with the commission.
>
> It is the first asking price.
>
> A home gets the most attention it will ever get in its first week or two on the market. That is
> when the active buyers, the agents with someone ready, and the algorithms all look at once. Price
> above what the market supports and you burn that window correcting course, then spend the next
> two months following the market down with reductions. Right now 36% of active San Clemente
> listings are carrying a price cut. Almost none of them planned to be there.
>
> The homes selling in under two weeks did one thing well. They launched at a number the market
> could say yes to.
>
> So the work happens before the sign goes in the yard, not after. Get a real read on what your
> home is worth in today's market, from someone who will tell you the honest number rather than the
> flattering one. That single decision moves more dollars than anything that happens later.
>
> If you want that read on your place, message me. No pressure either way.
