What I'm Watching as the 2026 Market Opens

After a slow December, January numbers are starting to come in. The pattern I keep seeing in San Clemente is the one that defined the back half of last year. Homes priced correctly from day one are clearing fast. Homes priced on the high side of their range are sitting. Buyer demand has not gone away, it has just gotten more selective. Rates softened slightly in the new year, which is helping move-up buyers feel less stuck. If you are considering a move in 2026, the next ninety days are when most of the spring decision-making happens. I would rather have a quiet conversation now than a rushed one in May.

What's Happening in the Neighborhood

This Issue  |  San Clemente

Hillcrest Is Where the Capital Is Going

Hillcrest, the hilltop neighborhood at the south end of San Clemente looking out over the ocean and Trestles, is the most active renovation pocket in the city right now. New builds, ground-up rebuilds, and full-scope remodels are advancing across multiple streets in parallel. When this much capital lands in one neighborhood at the same time, it tightens future inventory and pulls the comps with it.

Owners Are Reinvesting, Not Selling

Across the rest of South San Clemente, the dominant pattern is full-scope renovation, not cosmetic refresh. That is what owners do when they are planning to stay for a long run. It strengthens neighborhood comps and signals that the people already here are not the ones giving up their seats anytime soon.

What's New in San Clemente

Pure Burger Bar Opens in San Clemente

Pure Burger Bar quietly opened in town this month, a small organic, grass-fed burger concept with loaded fries and a focused menu. February openings tend to fly under the radar before the spring tourist mix shows up, which makes them worth catching early.

Pre-Spring Is When the Locals Get the South County Breaks Back

February is the quiet stretch at the south county surf spots. Trestles, T-Street, and Lower Trestles all thin out before the spring crowd returns. If you are looking at homes here from out of state, this is the part of the year that shows you what the town feels like day to day.
