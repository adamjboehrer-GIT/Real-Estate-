# San Clemente Market Brief — April 2026

**Source:** Pacific Sotheby's April 21, 2026 Market Report + CRMLS via InfoSparks (March 2026 SFR). PDF archived at `Newsletter/market_data/sources/2026-04-21_pacific_sothebys_deck.pdf`.

---

## The story in one sentence
San Clemente under $5M is one of the most efficient markets in Orange County right now — supply contracting, pending sales rising, sellers holding firm on price, and homes closing above list in 15 days.

## The three numbers that tell it

1. **Expected Market Time: 52 days.** Second-fastest in OC behind Aliso Viejo (47). Faster than Dana Point (74), Laguna Niguel (77), Newport Beach (149), Corona Del Mar (178), Laguna Beach (201).
2. **Sale-to-list ratio, March 2026: 100.8% overall; 101.1% under $5M.** SC buyers are bidding at or above list for well-priced homes.
3. **Only 28% of active SC listings have a price reduction.** Among the lowest in OC. For comparison: Laguna Niguel 37%, Newport Coast 35%, Irvine 34%, Laguna Beach 34%. Translation: SC sellers priced right the first time.

## Price band split (the nuance)
- **Under $5M (50 homes sold in March):** 101.1% S/L ratio, 15-day median DOM. This is the seller's leverage zone.
- **$5M and above (2 homes sold in March):** 97.2% S/L ratio, 74-day median DOM. The ultra-luxury segment is negotiating harder and sitting longer — this is consistent with what's happening in Corona Del Mar and Newport Coast at that tier.

## How SC sits long-term
- Zillow Home Value Index, 10-year growth: **+123.1%**. Top tier of Orange County.
- Peer set: Newport Coast +138.7%, Newport Beach +126.3%, Laguna Niguel +123.9%, Dana Point +122.5%, Laguna Beach +98.0%, SJC +104.9%.
- SC is sitting with Dana Point and Laguna Niguel at the top of the decade-growth league.

## National framing (for "Note From Adam" if used)
- 30-year fixed rate: ~6.32% (mid-April 2026, Mortgage News Daily). Down from 7%+ in 2024.
- NAR Realtor Confidence: 37% of agents expect buyer demand to rise over the next year (up from 27% a year ago).
- Seasonal pattern: March through June is peak offers (2.9 per listing) and fastest days-on-market (46 to 52 nationally).
- SC at 15-day median DOM is running well ahead of that national spring pace.
