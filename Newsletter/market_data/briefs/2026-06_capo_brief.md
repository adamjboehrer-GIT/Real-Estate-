## The one structural fact that shapes this issue
**Capistrano Beach is not a separate city.** It is the southern end of Dana Point (ZIP 92624), between Doheny and the San Clemente line at Poche Creek. Neither the Pacific Sotheby's deck nor InfoSparks breaks Capo out, so the hard market stats anchor to **Dana Point**, and the genuinely-Capo number is the Redfin 92624 price-per-foot. All the local color (neighborhood, lifestyle, intel) is Capo-specific, which is where Adam's credibility lives. Locally it is always "Capo Beach," never "Capistrano" (that is the mission town inland) — getting that right reads as local.

## The data story in one sentence
The everyday Capo/Dana Point market (single-family, under $5M) is still closing fast and near list — about 9 days on market and within a few points of asking — but the broader Dana Point market is visibly cooling this summer: expected market time stretched to 112 days (from 99 a year ago), roughly one in three listings is carrying a price cut, and the June deck's own outlook says demand has peaked and market time is lengthening.

## Dana Point — the recurring stats (Capo's home market)
**Single-family (InfoSparks, May 2026):**
- Median days on market **9** (down from 15 a year ago).
- Months of supply **3.6** (down from 4.4 a year ago; still a seller's market under six months).
- % of last list received **99.4%**; closed sales 23; SFR median **$3.45M** (thin sample, +40.8% YoY — DO NOT feature; small-count artifact).

**All residential (deck, June snapshot / May closings):**
- Snapshot: 93 active, 25 pendings, Expected Market Time **112 days** (last year 99), **31%** of listings with a price cut.
- Year over year: inventory -26% (-33 homes), demand -34% (-13 pendings), market time +12 days.
- May 2026 closings: 38 homes. All: **$2.0M** median, **93.1%** sale-to-list, 15 days. Under $5M: 33 homes, $1.7M, **95.7%**, 14 days. $5M+: 5 homes, $6.6M, 99.4%, 23 days.
- Zillow HVI (April 2026): +0.5% MoM, +3.8% YoY, **+114.7%** over ten years.

## Capistrano Beach (92624) specific
- Price per sq ft ~**$934**, up ~16% YoY (Redfin) — the steadiest read in a low-volume coastal ZIP.
- Median sale price is mix-driven: inland flats trade roughly $1.3M–$1.6M, Palisades bluff $2.0M–$2.6M (e.g., 26757 Calle Maria sold $2,565,000 Sept 2025), Beach Road oceanfront $3M+. Blended ZIP median bounces $1.4M–$2.4M month to month — present directionally, not as a hard headline.
- Sub-$1.5M coastal single-family in the flats is the rarest, fastest-moving product.

## National framing (deck June outlook)
Existing-home sales +0.2% MoM (April). Affordability index 110.6 (vs 101.4 a year ago). First-time buyers 33%. Mortgage rates mid-6%. Supply climbing toward its July–August summer peak; demand has peaked and Expected Market Time is lengthening. Per standing guidance: no rate-pressure or ARM angle to frame sellers. Usable macro line: the market is normalizing into summer, so well-priced and well-presented wins, and buyers have regained some negotiating room.
