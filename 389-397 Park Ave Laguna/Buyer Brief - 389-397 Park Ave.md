## Critical Unknowns (Verify Before Offer)

These five variables determine which scenarios are real. Treat any pricing decision as conditional until verified.

1. **STR legality at this parcel.** Laguna Beach banned new STRs in residential zones (R-1, R-2, R-3) in 2022, ratified by Coastal Commission. Existing permitted STL units in those zones are legal nonconforming and transferable. STRs are still allowed in CBD-1, CBD-2, CBD Central Bluffs, CBD Office, CBD Visitor Commercial, plus LB/P, C-N, C-1, CH-M zones with Administrative or Conditional Use Permit. Park Ave between Forest and Glenneyre runs through the Downtown Specific Plan boundary, so the parcel could be CBD-zoned. Listing categorizes the property as "Single Family Residence (Detached)" which suggests R-1, but that is a sub-type field, not a zoning field. **Action: confirm zoning with the City Community Development Department and ask if the parcel has an existing STL permit.**

2. **Historic register status and Mills Act eligibility.** Listing claims "potential historic designations and possible Mills Act tax benefits." Laguna's Mills Act program is restricted to "E" (Excellent) or "K" (Key) rated structures on the City's Historic Register. The 1932 LA Olympics relocation story is compelling but the property is not yet designated. Designation + Mills Act contract typically takes 6-12 months. Savings, once in place, are 40-60% on property tax annually. **Action: confirm historic rating status with City. Cost to apply ~$1,500.**

3. **ADU permit status.** Plans submitted, not yet issued. Coastal OC ADU construction is $250-450/sqft base plus 15-20% coastal premium. Expect $200K-$280K all-in for the 533 sqft detached ADU. **Action: get current permit status and estimated time to RTI (ready-to-issue) from seller's architect.**

4. **In-place lease terms.** Both cottages occupied. Whether month-to-month or fixed-term determines whether a new buyer can re-lease at market, owner-occupy, or convert to STR/MTR. **Action: request copies of leases in disclosures.**

5. **Market vs. in-place rents.** Current $92,400 combined ($4,000/mo for 2BR, $3,700/mo for 1BR) is plausibly 10-20% under market for renovated walk-to-beach Laguna Village. Sotheby's MLS comp pull would confirm.

## Tax & Depreciation Side

Often the deal that doesn't pencil on cap rate pencils on after-tax IRR. The OBBBA, signed July 2025, **permanently restored 100% bonus depreciation** for property placed in service after January 20, 2025. This materially changes the buyer pool.

### Cost segregation, year 1 depreciation

At $2.5M acquisition + $250K ADU = $2.75M basis. Coastal CA land allocation typically 25-30%; use 25% defensible = $688K land (not depreciable), $2,062K depreciable building basis. A small-property engineered cost seg study (cost $2,500-$5,000) typically reclassifies:

| Asset class | Life | % of building basis | Dollar amount | Year 1 with 100% bonus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5-year personal property (cabinets, appliances, fixtures, carpet, window treatments) | 5 | 25% | $516K | $516K |
| 15-year land improvements (landscaping, fences, decks, driveway) | 15 | 12% | $247K | $247K |
| 27.5-year structure (walls, roof, foundation, structural HVAC/electrical) | 27.5 | 63% | $1,299K | $47K (straight-line) |
| **Total year 1 depreciation** | | | | **~$810K** |

### STR loophole vs LTR passive treatment

For STR (average stay <7 days) with **material participation** (100+ hours and more than any other person, or 500+ hours), the rental income is treated as **active**, not passive. The $810K of paper losses can offset W-2 income, business income, or any ordinary income.

For LTR or MTR, losses are passive and only offset other passive income, unless the owner qualifies as a Real Estate Professional (REPS: 750+ hours and >50% of work hours in real estate).

At a 50% combined marginal rate (37% federal top bracket + ~10-13% CA effective), $810K of year 1 deductions is worth ~$405K in actual tax savings.

### 1031 exchange mechanics

For a buyer with trapped gains in another investment property:

- 45 calendar days from sale of relinquished property to identify replacement(s) in writing to the Qualified Intermediary. Strict, no extensions.
- 180 calendar days (or buyer's tax return due date, whichever is earlier) to close.
- Replacement value must be equal to or greater than relinquished value to defer all tax. Any shortfall ("boot") is taxed.
- Must use a Qualified Intermediary; buyer cannot touch the cash.

**Example trapped-gains buyer:** Sold a $3M Newport Beach duplex (basis $800K, $400K accumulated depreciation). Tax exposure without 1031: ~$970K (20% federal cap gains + 25% depreciation recapture + 3.8% NIIT + 13.3% CA state). That $970K is what the deal is competing against.

For that buyer to fully defer, they need to deploy $3M into replacement property. Park Ave at $2.5M + $250K ADU + $100K cap-ex absorbs $2.85M; they'd take $150K of boot (taxed ~$60-75K) or pair Park Ave with a smaller second property. Park Ave at $2.7M list + $250K ADU + $100K cap-ex = $3.05M fully absorbs.

### Combined tax value vs. weak cap rate

For the 1031 + STR + cost seg buyer at $2.7M:

- 1031 defers ~$970K tax on relinquished property
- Year 1 cost seg + 100% bonus depreciation: ~$810K paper losses
- Year 1 federal+state tax savings (50% marginal): ~$405K
- Mills Act savings if approved: $15K/yr × 10 years discounted = ~$110K NPV
- **Combined year 1 tax value: ~$1,375K on a $2.95M total deployment**

A 3% cap rate is irrelevant. The IRR is being driven by tax deferral, not operating yield.

### Caveats

- **Depreciation gets recaptured at sale.** Bonus depreciation comes back as ordinary income up to 25% on exit. It is a deferral, not a permanent benefit, unless the buyer 1031s again or holds to death (step-up basis).
- **STR loophole requires real material participation.** If buyer outsources to full-service PM and never logs hours, IRS will challenge. Need contemporaneous time logs.
- **Land allocation matters.** Aggressive 40%+ land allocation by IRS auditor would shrink depreciable base. Defend with engineered study and comparable land sales.
- **OBBBA is the floor for 2026, not the ceiling.** Bonus depreciation is now permanent at 100%, but future Congress could change it. Window is favorable through at least the current administration.

## Negotiation Levers (Given Seller Relationship)

Since acquisition price flexes the model significantly, sub-list paths to test:

1. **Seller carry at 5.5%, 5-7 year balloon.** If seller has low basis, installment-sale tax treatment defers gains and gives the buyer 100-200bps below market rate. Worth ~$50K-$80K/yr to the buyer over the carry period.
2. **Mills Act contingency credit.** Buyer accepts price contingent on seller cooperating with historic designation application post-close. Credit at close to offset ~$1,500 application fee plus 6-12 months of standard property tax until Mills Act flips.
3. **ADU permit issuance credit.** Plans are submitted, not approved. Negotiate $15K-$25K credit if RTI not in hand at close, since buyer carries permit risk.
4. **Tenant termination cooperation.** If leases are fixed-term, buyer's ability to convert to STR/MTR is locked. Negotiate seller-funded tenant buyouts or extended close to align with lease expiry.
5. **Drop list ~7% to $2.50M.** Already cut $215K, 92 DOM, tenant-occupied 24-hr-notice slows showings, $2,718/sqft is aggressive even for Laguna Village. Comparable cottage land has traded $1.8M-$2.2M.

## What Has to Be True for This Deal to Pencil

In rank order:

1. **STR legality or grandfathered permit transferable.** This is the single largest swing variable. STR-legal moves cap rate from 3% range to 7% range.
2. **ADU built within 12 months of close.** $46K-$72K of additional gross income is necessary to hit any reasonable yield threshold.
3. **Acquisition price at or below $2.5M.** $180K of negotiation upside is the difference between negative and positive leverage with financing.
4. **Mills Act executed within 18 months.** $15K/yr forever after, compounding.
5. **Top-quartile operating execution.** Average Laguna STR returns $72K/listing/yr. Top quartile returns $140K/yr. Operator skill, design, photography, pricing strategy account for most of the spread.

If any of the first three fail, this deal does not pencil as an income property at list price. It pencils only as a lifestyle purchase, a 1031 vehicle, or a long-hold land bank.
